Responsible AI — How this works safely

Risk: The AI may mispredict on unusual days (field trips, snow days, menu changes).

Mitigation: Predictions show context. The AI does not place orders — it recommends. Maria (the cafeteria manager) makes the final call. She can override anytime.

Human-in-the-Loop: The AI does not decide the final order quantity. A human reviews the recommendation and considers factors the AI can't see — leftover stock, supplier issues, student field trips.

The Problem

Meet Maria, a cafeteria manager at Lincoln High School. Every Monday, she makes 300 chicken tenders — but only ~180 students buy them. The other 120 servings go straight in the trash. That's $180 and 52 kg of CO₂ wasted every week on just one menu item.

Maria has no way to predict waste. She orders based on gut feel — but every day is different. Pizza Tuesdays sell out. Fish Stick Fridays flop. She needs AI that learns the patterns she can't see.

How the AI Works

1

Input — Day, menu item, portions, weather, holidays, events

2

AI Processing — A Random Forest model finds hidden patterns (e.g., "Chicken Tenders on a rainy Monday = 38% waste")

3

Output — Predicted waste in servings + cost + CO₂ + recommendation

4

Action — Maria adjusts her order, saves money, reduces waste

Why AI? A simple average won't catch weather × day × menu interactions. AI finds the hidden patterns.
